30

May 9, 2025

Today, I turn 30. No loud fireworks, no profound transformation. Just a quiet pulse of time moving forward. A soft exhale.

I thought I might do something big — a tattoo maybe, a symbol of change etched onto my skin. But instead, I find myself wanting to be — still, unrushed, and honest.

There’s nothing wrong with celebration. But today, I don’t want noise. I want clarity. I want to ask myself: Who am I building? Not for the world, not for validation — but for me. For the woman I meet in the mirror when no one else is watching.

I’ve crossed many thresholds already — of love, of longing, of letting go. I’ve found a partner who makes life soft and safe. I’ve survived the ache of becoming and the quiet victories of healing. And now, as I step into this decade, I am not trying to reinvent myself. I am trying to return — to the core of who I’ve always been.

Today is not about doing. It’s about witnessing. Not proving, but choosing. Choosing peace. Choosing purpose. Choosing presence.

So here’s to 30 — not as a turning point, but as a grounding point. To the garden I’m planting within. To the years ahead — slow, intentional, real.

I don’t need fireworks. I am the spark.
