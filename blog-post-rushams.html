RuShams: The Eternal Dance of Love and Light

March 22, 2025

Growing up, my first introduction to Rumi was through the chapters of history books on the Sufi movement. Even then, I sensed that his words carried something beyond mere poetry — they carried a love that was not bound by human conventions, but one that expanded infinitely, embracing life, experience, and the cosmos itself. However, it wasn’t until I read The Forty Rules of Love by Elif Shafak that I truly discovered the essence of Rumi’s transformation, through his companionship with Shams of Tabriz.

Rumi was already a respected scholar and teacher when Shams walked into his life. But it was this wandering dervish, unpredictable and passionate, who shattered the walls of conventional wisdom and reawakened a fire in Rumi’s soul. Their companionship was not that of mere friends or teacher and disciple — it was a meeting of souls, a merging of minds and spirits on a level that transcended words. Shams became the mirror in which Rumi saw the infinite reflection of divine love.

“Where love dissolves the self, and souls intertwine in the infinite dance of light and wisdom — RuShams, an eternal embrace beyond time.”
“Be like a river in generosity and help, like the sun in compassion and mercy, like the night in covering others’ faults, like the dead in anger and fury, like the earth in humility and modesty.” — Rumi

Reading about their journey, I found myself in awe of the kind of love and companionship that exists beyond possession, beyond labels. It is a love that liberates rather than binds, a companionship that challenges rather than comforts, and a devotion that stretches towards the eternal. Theirs was not just a human relationship — it was an encounter that expanded consciousness, rewrote definitions, and created poetry that continues to stir hearts centuries later.

“Do not seek love, seek the barriers you have built against it and remove them.” — Shams of Tabriz

Ever since, my own understanding of love and companionship has been shaped by their story. It led me to seek not just a partner in life, but a soul who understands my thoughts, my silences, and my yearning for something beyond the material world. I found that love in my partner — gentle and humble like Rumi, embodying wisdom, humility, compassion, and a boundless love for all, believing in spreading joy and kindness without limits.

How marvelous it is to exist in this fleeting glimpse of time, to have the eyes to see, the words to express, and the heart to feel. To love is the greatest act of being alive, and to recognize a love like that of Rumi and Shams—even in fragments, even in passing—is to touch the very fabric of the universe.

What a gift it is to be alive and to love.
